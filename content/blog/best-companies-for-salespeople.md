Sales is one of the few roles where the company you work for matters as much as your skill. Great salespeople in broken environments still underperform. Average salespeople in the right environment break records.

The difference comes down to a few things: a product that sells itself (or at least doesn't fight you), a comp plan that rewards performance, leadership that came up through sales rather than around it, and a pipeline of real opportunity.

We looked at the companies where salespeople consistently report strong earnings, clear career ladders, and management that actually knows what good looks like.

## What makes a company great for salespeople

Before the list: the criteria.

**Quota attainability.** A comp plan is fiction if quota is unachievable. The best companies set quotas where top performers can realistically hit 120-150% and average performers can hit 90-100%. Watch out for companies that ratchet quota up every year without adjusting OTE.

**Product-market fit.** Cold calling is brutal when you don't believe in the product. The companies on this list sell things people genuinely want — the inbound pipeline is real, and customers renew.

**Comp structure.** Base/OTE ratios, uncapped commission, accelerators above quota, and fast payment cycles. The best companies don't make you wait 90 days to see your W-2.

**Career trajectory.** SDR → AE → Senior AE → Enterprise AE → Sales Manager is a well-worn path at the best organizations. The companies worth your time have made it before and can name names.

**Culture from the top.** Companies where the CEO came up through sales, or where revenue leadership has direct P&L accountability, tend to invest in their reps. Companies where sales is an afterthought to product don't.

## The companies

### Salesforce
The mother church of B2B SaaS sales. Salesforce invented the modern enterprise sales playbook and has been refining it for 25 years. The training program (especially for new AEs) is among the best in the industry — methodical, process-driven, and built around genuine skill development rather than just hustle.

OTE is strong at the enterprise level, and the brand carries weight on a resume. The downside: territory is increasingly competitive, and the company is mature enough that you're selling against a long-established (and sometimes jaded) install base.

**Best for:** Reps who want elite training and a recognizable brand. A strong first or second stop in enterprise sales.

---

### HubSpot
One of the most rep-friendly environments in SaaS. HubSpot is famous for strong enablement, clear career progression, and a product that generates real inbound interest. The INBOUND marketing motion means reps are often working warm leads rather than cold outbound.

Quota attainment rates have historically been above industry average, and the culture emphasizes coaching over policing. Salespeople consistently cite strong management and collaborative team culture.

**Best for:** SMB and mid-market reps. An exceptional place to develop fundamentals early in a sales career.

---

### Gong
If you want to work with the product that's reshaping how sales teams operate, Gong is the place. The company sells conversation intelligence and revenue intelligence — tools that salespeople care about — and the mission resonates with enterprise buyers in a way most software doesn't.

Growth has been significant, and the enterprise AE role comes with meaningful OTE and an expanding market. The culture is intense but the best reps thrive here.

**Best for:** Mid-market and enterprise AEs who want to sell to other sales leaders. Strong comp, strong product story.

---

### Outreach
One of the original sales engagement platforms and still one of the category leaders. Outreach has a seasoned sales motion, real enterprise presence, and strong compensation for top performers.

The sales team is known for being disciplined and process-driven — this isn't a culture of throwing things at the wall. Reps who like methodical, account-based approaches tend to excel here.

**Best for:** Enterprise AEs and SDRs who want structure and a clear career ladder. Strong brand recognition in the RevOps world.

---

### Salesloft
The other sales engagement heavyweight, with a strong foothold in enterprise accounts and a culture that treats salespeople well. Salesloft has made consistent investments in sales enablement and leadership development, and the team has a reputation for excellent management.

Post-merger with Drift (and backed by Vista Equity), the combined company is one of the larger pure-play revenue platforms in the market.

**Best for:** AEs who want enterprise exposure at a company that takes sales culture seriously.

---

### Apollo.io
One of the fastest-growing sales intelligence platforms on the market. Apollo combines prospecting data, sequencing, and CRM in one tool — and the product has genuine momentum, especially in the mid-market.

The sales team is scrappy and high-velocity. Reps who thrive in a faster-paced, outbound-heavy environment will do well here. Equity upside is real given the trajectory.

**Best for:** Reps who want to grow with a company that's still in its up-and-to-the-right phase.

---

### Seismic
The market leader in sales enablement. Seismic's product touches every enterprise sales team — it's the platform that houses pitch decks, battle cards, and content reps actually use in deals.

The enterprise sales motion is sophisticated, and the average deal size is significant. Reps here develop strong discovery and business-case skills because the buyers are senior.

**Best for:** Enterprise AEs who want to sell a well-established platform to C-suite buyers. Strong comp at the enterprise level.

---

### Highspot
Seismic's primary competitor and close behind in market share. Highspot has a strong culture of internal promotion and rep development, and the company is at an interesting stage — large enough to have credibility, still growing fast enough to offer upside.

The sales organization has a reputation for excellent managers, which matters more than most people acknowledge when evaluating opportunities.

**Best for:** AEs who prioritize management quality and culture alongside comp.

---

### Clari
Revenue intelligence at the enterprise level. Clari's platform is used by CROs and VP of Sales to forecast revenue — which means Clari's own reps are selling to people who understand sales, and can appreciate a well-run process.

The sales motion is consultative and deal cycles are longer, but deal sizes are meaningful. Reps with a knack for executive conversation tend to do well.

**Best for:** Senior AEs and enterprise reps who want to work in a smaller, focused team with strong upside.

---

### LinkedIn
LinkedIn's Sales Solutions division (Sales Navigator, recruiter tools) is one of the more underrated places to build a sales career. The product has genuine network effects and near-universal adoption, which means you're rarely convincing someone that your space matters — just that your tier is worth it.

The comp structure is strong, the brand is irreproachable, and the company is large enough to offer internal mobility. The trade-off is that innovation cycles are slow and the bureaucracy of a Microsoft subsidiary is real.

**Best for:** Reps who want strong comp and stability at a recognized brand. Particularly good for reps who like solution-selling over pure new business hunting.

---

### Rippling
One of the fastest-growing software companies in the world. Rippling's compound product (HR, IT, and Finance in one platform) creates a sales motion unlike almost anything else in the market — each deal is a real expansion opportunity, and the ACV grows as customers add modules.

The culture is demanding but the comp is top-tier and the product sells itself at a remarkable rate. Leadership came up through sales (Parker Conrad is deeply engaged with the revenue motion) and it shows.

**Best for:** High-output AEs who want to be part of a breakout company. Expect to work hard — and to earn accordingly.

---

### Brex
One of the best places in fintech to build a sales career. Brex targets CFOs and finance teams with spend management and corporate cards, which means the buyers are senior and the deals are complex. The company has been through some strategic pivots (pulling back from SMB, doubling down on enterprise) and has emerged with a sharper motion.

OTE is competitive, the brand is strong in startup and growth-stage circles, and the internal culture takes sales seriously.

**Best for:** AEs who want fintech exposure and to work with senior financial buyers. Good stepping stone toward enterprise financial software.

---

### Stripe
One of the most respected sales environments in tech. Stripe is deliberate about who it hires — reps tend to be more technically fluent than average, because the buyers are developers and CTOs rather than procurement teams.

The sales motion is consultative and often involves multi-month deals with significant complexity. Compensation reflects this: OTE is among the highest in the industry for enterprise roles. The brand is strong in ways that compound over time on your resume.

**Best for:** Technical AEs and enterprise reps who want to sell at the highest level. Less for volume-hungry closers, more for relationship-driven deal-makers.

---

### Monday.com
A strong mid-market sales environment with genuine product-market fit across dozens of verticals. Monday's work OS has broad applicability, which means AEs develop range — selling to ops teams, marketing, IT, and everything in between.

The company is well-run, publicly traded, and has a culture that treats the sales org as a first-class team. OTE is solid, progression is clear, and the product genuinely helps customers.

**Best for:** AEs who want a product that sells across verticals with a collaborative internal culture.

---

### Zendesk
A proven enterprise sales environment. Zendesk sells customer service and CX software to companies of every size, and the enterprise team handles large, complex deals with Fortune 500 buyers.

The company has been through a PE acquisition (Permira and Hellman & Friedman), which has changed the operating environment — leaner, more focused on profitability. The upside: sharper focus and clear accountability. The trade-off: slower hiring and fewer internal resources than during the hypergrowth years.

**Best for:** Enterprise AEs who want a proven environment with senior buyers and real deal complexity.

---

### Twilio
Twilio's sales motion is unusual — the company built on developer-led growth, which means many deals start bottom-up and AEs layer in expansion and formalization. Reps who understand technical products and can navigate both developer champions and business buyers do exceptionally well.

The enterprise motion has matured significantly, and the average deal size has grown. The stock has been through a rough cycle, but the underlying business is large and the product is deeply embedded.

**Best for:** Technical AEs who like the developer-led growth model and expansion-focused selling.

## What to look for when evaluating an offer

**Ask about quota attainment.** What percentage of reps hit quota last year? What was the median attainment? If they won't give you a number, that's a number.

**Ask about territory.** How is territory assigned? How often does it change? Are there protected accounts or will you be competing against existing relationships?

**Ask about the pipeline.** What percentage of business is inbound vs. outbound? What does the average sales cycle look like? How long have the top reps been there?

**Ask about the plan.** Get the comp plan in writing before you accept. Understand exactly how commission is calculated, when it's paid, and what happens to in-flight deals if you leave or are let go.

**Talk to current reps.** Not references they give you — people you find on LinkedIn. The gap between what you hear in the interview and what you hear from people who actually work there is usually telling.

---

The best sales job isn't just about OTE. It's about a product that gives you a fair shot, a manager who invests in your development, and a culture where high performance is recognized and rewarded. The companies on this list check those boxes — most of the time, for the right rep.

Do your diligence. But if you land at any of these, you're starting from a good position.
